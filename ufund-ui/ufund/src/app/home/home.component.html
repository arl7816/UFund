About Us

We at Society for the Welfare of Endangered Nestlings are dedicated to preserving avian wildlife around the globe. We work with local communities to restore natural habitats and resources, aid injured nestlings, support restoration ecology, and -- most importantly -- to uplift local bird communities. Established in 2024 by software-engineers/bird-watchers, SWEN has made an impact in bird populations across North America, and we intend to spread our wings and help nestlings around the globe.

Organization News
Puffin Preservation

We are working with officials at the National Park Service to support local puffin populations in Maine by reducing traffic near their habitats and removing polutants from the water.

Geese and Government

In partnership with a local bird-watching club, SWEN is in negotiations with city officials in Rochester, NY to establish a new state park where geese can rest during migration.

Dodo De-Extinction?

SWEN is supporting new genetic research that could help restore endangered or extinct populations, including the famous Dodo.
